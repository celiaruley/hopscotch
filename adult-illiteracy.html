Illiteracy is the inability to read or write. This looks different for every individual and can vary across cultures.

Reading and writing give us tools that let us share and communicate ideas, stories, knowledge and learn other languages. Despite how reading and writing go hand in hand, there is not one way or one combination that universally teaches everyone how to read.

43 million adults in the United States cannot read, write, or do basic math above a third-grade level.

If all adults had the equivalent of a sixth grade reading level, it would generate an additional $2.2 trillion in annual income for the United States. However, due to a decreases in federal and state funding, less then 10% of adults in need of this education receive services.

Learning to read, write, do basic math and use computers allow an individual more opportunity to find and keep employment and stay out of poverty.

Globally, there are 773 million illiterate adults, most of which are women. As a result, a mother’s reading skills play a huge factor in their child’s academic success.

Community-based literacy programs offer instruction in basic literacy, GED prep, English, citizenship, job readiness, financial literacy, digital literacy, health literacy, drivers license prep & more. These programs offer individuals the skills needed to better support themselves. Accomodations such as audio books and computer access allow folx with learning difficulties support as well.
